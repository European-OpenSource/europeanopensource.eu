Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 22, 2024

This notice describes how European OpenSource (europeanopensource.eu) collects and processes users' data through the use of TWIPLA (twipla.com) for website analytics and Brevo (brevo.com) for newsletter management.

1. Data Controller

The data controller for this website is Fabrizio Cafolla, acting as the maintainer of the European OpenSource project (a personal, non-commercial initiative). For any privacy-related requests or to exercise your rights, contact:
support \[@\] europeanopensource \[.\] eu

2. Data Collected

2.1 Website Analytics (TWIPLA)
  • Anonymous statistical data: We use TWIPLA in its privacy-first configuration. It tracks traffic and collects technical details (operating system, browser type, country) without using cookies or storing IP addresses in a way that allows direct or indirect identification.
  • Information collected: Page visit count, session duration, and referrer. This data is processed in aggregate form and is not associated with any specific individual.
2.2 Newsletter (Brevo)
  • Email address: Collected upon your explicit subscription.
  • Engagement data: We monitor delivery rates, opening rates, and link clicks to improve our content. This data is associated with your email address via tracking pixels provided by Brevo.

3. Purpose and Legal Basis of Processing

3.1 Website Analytics

Processing is based on your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), requested via banner upon your first visit. If consent is not given, TWIPLA analytics are not activated.

  • Website performance monitoring and improvement.
  • Understanding visitor behavior through aggregated, non-identifiable metrics.
3.2 Newsletter

Processing is based on your explicit consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR). You provide consent by submitting the subscription form. You can withdraw this consent at any time via the "unsubscribe" link.

4. Cookies

TWIPLA does not use cookies for analytics. Our website only uses essential technical cookies (e.g., to remember your consent preferences). No profiling or marketing cookies are used.

5. Data Recipients and Retention

  • TWIPLA: Data is processed within the EU. Retention: max 14 months.
  • Brevo (Sendinblue SAS): Our newsletter service provider based in France. They act as a Data Processor under a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA). Your email address and engagement data are retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion. After unsubscription, data is deleted within 1 day, except where retention is required by law (e.g., for legal claims or accounting purposes).
  • We do not sell or share your data with any other third parties.

6. Users' Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your newsletter data:

  • Access and Rectification: Request a copy or correction of your data.
  • Erasure (Right to be forgotten): Request permanent deletion of your email.
  • Restriction of Processing: Request that we limit how we use your data.
  • Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format.
  • Withdrawal of Consent: Unsubscribe at any time.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You can contact your local Data Protection Authority.

To exercise these rights, email: support \[@\] europeanopensource \[.\] eu

7. Data Security and Transfers

We use encrypted connections (HTTPS) and industry-standard tools. Brevo stores data primarily in the EU. For any secondary transfers outside the EEA, Brevo ensures compliance through Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).

8. Changes to this Policy

We may update this notice to reflect changes in our tools or legal requirements. Significant changes will be notified via the newsletter.
